About Julianna

Originally from Orange County, CA, Julianna Conley is a MSJ candidate in Northwestern's Medill School of Journalism, specializing in magazine writing. She graduated cum laude from the University of Notre Dame's honors program with majors in Sociology and Pre-Health Studies and a minor in Journalism, Ethics, and Democracy.

After completing her undergraduate studies, she taught 4th grade in Sacramento for two years through AmeriCorps, while earning her Master's of Education through Notre Dame's ACE Teaching Fellowship. In 2024, she moved to Chicago to teach 3rd grade STEM in Chicago Public Schools.

A versatile writer, she's written copy for newsletters, creative short stories for academic journals, PR articles for university relations teams, and a consistently-trending column for her college newspaper. She's also developed multiple podcasts, two of which won "Best Podcast" and "3rd Best Podcast" from the Indiana Collegiate Press Awards.
